## 2. Analysis of Impacts

### 2.1 Employment & Task Shift: The '88% Rule'
We are observing a massive divergence in how work is valued. According to **Learning People (2026)**, roles exposed to AI in the Australian market have seen their core skill requirements shift by **88%** since 2024. In sectors such as banking and hospitality, AI is not replacing the "worker," but replacing the "data-heavy tasks" within the job. This allows for **Augmentation**, where humans spend more time on high-value client interaction and strategic problem-solving.

 a **"Digital Divide"** for regional Australia and SME-heavy industries like manufacturing. Small businesses lack the capital to invest in bespoke AI models, potentially leading to a decline in their competitive position relative to multinational corporations (Oxford Economics 2026).
